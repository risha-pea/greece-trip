Where to Eat

Restaurants & Tavernas

Athens ¬∑ Plaka
Scholarchio
Traditional Ouzeri & Mezedopoleio

Hidden in a Plaka courtyard, this classic ouzeri serves dozens of small mezedes ‚Äî grilled octopus, taramosalata, spanakopita, saganaki. Order the house ouzo and share everything. A true Athens institution.

Athens ¬∑ Monastiraki
Tzitzikas ke Mermigas
Modern Greek, Creative Mezedes

Cheerful, colourful restaurant serving inventive takes on Greek classics. The sun-dried tomato feta dip and the slow-cooked lamb are unmissable. Ideal before or after the Acropolis.

Meteora ¬∑ Kalambaka
Taverna Panellinio
Mountain Taverna, Local Produce

At the foot of the rocks, this family taverna has been feeding pilgrims and travellers for decades. The grilled lamb chops with mountain herbs and local trahanas soup are exceptional after a day of hiking.

Delphi
Taverna Dion
Greek Taverna, Valley Views

10 minutes from the ancient site with views down the olive-studded valley toward the Gulf of Corinth. The souvlaki and local Fokida wine are the ideal end to a day communing with Apollo.

Nafplio
Aiolos
Seafood & Peloponnese Cuisine

On the old harbour waterfront with views of Bourtzi Castle. Order whatever fresh fish came in that day, paired with Nemean Agiorgitiko ‚Äî the great red wine of the Peloponnese. Linger here for your farewell dinner.

Nafplio ¬∑ Old Town
Byzantio
Traditional Peloponnese Kitchen

Tucked into a neoclassical building in the old town, Byzantio serves slow-cooked regional dishes: stifado rabbit stew, stuffed aubergines, the local loukoumades honey fritters. Authentic and deeply satisfying.
